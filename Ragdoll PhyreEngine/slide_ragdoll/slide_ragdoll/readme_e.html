Ragdoll Tutorial : Slide Behavior

Overview

This tutorial shows how to control rolling behavior when a ragdoll is on a slope.

Result

Explanation

A trigger rigid body is used to check interactions between the ragdoll and the terrain, and it will be triggered when the Slide attribute has been specified for a surface (wall) where a collision occurred. An operation where a ragdoll rolls along a hill is represented. When the ragdoll is placed as-is at the top of the hill, in almost all cases it will slide down. Even if the collision terrain is actually flat, there are many cases where the rendering terrain represents fine bumps, and simply sliding down will seem visually unnatural. Therefore, an operation is applied where the ragdoll falls down the hill while rolling at the specified angular velocity. In order for smooth rolling, the orientation is rolled while the torso axis of the ragdoll is kept parallel to the hill, and translational velocity that corresponds with the inclination of the hill is applied for a smooth fall. When a ragdoll is stuck on complex terrain, it is also possible to use Slide to roll the ragdoll to a safe area for the purpose of avoiding this situation.
